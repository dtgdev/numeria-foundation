

The Numeria Platform

Status: Foundational
Version: 0.1

Purpose

The Numeria Platform is the semantic and technological foundation that powers the Numeria Educational IP.

It connects mathematical truth, characters, stories, curriculum, media, assessment, and personalized learning through one coherent knowledge architecture.

Mission

Create a semantic learning platform where knowledge is authored once and experienced everywhere.

Platform Principle

Author knowledge once. Experience it everywhere.

A mathematical concept should not be independently redefined in books, games, lessons, animations, and AI prompts.

Instead, those experiences reference a shared canonical representation.

It contains entities such as:

* Mathematical Concept
* Character
* Story
* Lesson
* Curriculum

* Assessment
* Media Asset
* Location

It also contains explicit relationships such as:

* represents
* teaches
* requires
* appearsIn
* partOf
* illustrates
* explains
* prerequisiteFor
* relatedTo
* adaptedAs
* assessedBy

Truth Separation

The Platform distinguishes between three important forms of truth.

Mathematical Truth

Facts that are true within mathematics.

Example:

A derivative describes instantaneous rate of change.

Canonical Universe Truth

Official facts about the Numeria universe.

Example:

Detective Derivative represents the derivative concept.

Story Truth

Facts that are true within a specific narrative.

Example:

Detective Derivative investigated the Mystery of the Missing Slope.

These layers must remain distinguishable so imaginative storytelling never becomes confused with mathematical fact.

Entity Lifecycle

Every managed entity follows a versioned lifecycle.

Draft
  ↓
Review
  ↓
Canonical
  ↓
Revised
  ↓
Deprecated
  ↓
Archived

No important history should be lost.

Stable Identity

Every canonical entity receives a permanent identifier.

Examples:

NUM-CON-000001
NUM-CHR-000001
NUM-STO-000001
NUM-LES-000001

Names and descriptions may evolve.

Stable identifiers remain unchanged.

Provenance

Every canonical entity should eventually record:

* Creator
* Contributors
* Creation date
* Update date
* Version
* Review status
* Approval history
* Mathematical sources
* Educational sources
* Related decisions
* Superseded versions

AI Responsibilities

AI may assist with:

* Personalized explanations
* Story adaptation
* Question generation
* Lesson recommendations
* Translation
* Accessibility
* Misconception detection
* Content discovery
* Draft creation
* Tutor conversations

AI must not silently redefine canonical mathematics or canon.

Generated outputs should be grounded in approved knowledge and clearly separated from canonical source material until reviewed.

Platform Boundaries

The Platform manages knowledge and delivery systems.

It does not own the mission.

It does not independently define the creative canon.

It supports and connects the Foundation and the Educational IP.
