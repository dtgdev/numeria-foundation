# Numeria Relationship System

**Version:** 1.0 — Genesis

---

# Purpose

Relationships are the connections between Entities.

While Entities define *what* exists, Relationships define *how* everything in the Numeria universe is connected.

Understanding grows through relationships.

No Entity should exist in isolation.

---

# Philosophy

Mathematics is not a collection of independent facts.

It is a connected web of ideas.

Likewise, stories are built upon relationships between characters, places, artifacts, and events.

The Relationship System unifies mathematical, educational, and narrative connections into a single canonical model.

---

# Relationship Principles

Every relationship should be:

- Meaningful
- Intentional
- Canonical
- Understandable
- Bidirectional when appropriate

Relationships should improve understanding.

They should never exist merely for complexity.

---

# Relationship Categories

## Mathematical Relationships

Describe formal mathematical connections.

Examples:

- depends_on
- generalizes
- specializes
- proves
- derives_from
- transforms_into
- approximates
- equivalent_to
- inverse_of

---

## Educational Relationships

Describe learning progression.

Examples:

- introduces
- explains
- reinforces
- requires
- extends
- summarizes
- reviews
- prepares_for

---

## Character Relationships

Describe social interactions.

Examples:

- friend_of
- mentor_of
- student_of
- sibling_of
- rival_of
- protects
- guides
- inspires

---

## Story Relationships

Describe narrative structure.

Examples:

- appears_in
- discovers
- creates
- defeats
- rescues
- joins
- travels_to
- unlocks

---

## World Relationships

Describe geography and organization.

Examples:

- located_in
- governs
- belongs_to
- connected_to
- guards
- contains

---

# Cardinality

Relationships may be:

- One-to-One
- One-to-Many
- Many-to-One
- Many-to-Many

The model should support all four without limitation.

---

# Relationship Metadata

Every relationship should define:

- Unique ID
- Relationship Type
- Source Entity
- Target Entity
- Description
- Canon Status
- Educational Purpose
- Story Context
- Created Date
- Last Updated

---

# Visual Representation

Relationships should be recognizable at a glance.

Examples:

Solid Line

Canonical relationship

Dashed Line

Reference or temporary relationship

Animated Line

Active interaction

Curved Line

Narrative connection

Straight Line

Structural connection

Visual consistency is essential.

---

# Relationship Colors

Recommended semantic colors:

Blue

Knowledge

Green

Learning

Purple

Transformation

Orange

Discovery

Gold

Mentorship

Red

Conflict

Grey

Reference

These meanings should remain consistent throughout the Numeria ecosystem.

---

# Relationship Strength

Relationships may include strength.

Examples:

Strong

Core mathematical dependency

Medium

Frequently associated

Weak

Occasional connection

This enables richer graph exploration.

---

# AI Usage

Artificial intelligence should understand relationships as first-class knowledge.

AI should answer questions such as:

"What concepts depend on Limits?"

"Who mentored Derivative?"

"What stories introduce Integrals?"

"What must be learned before Differential Equations?"

Relationships make these answers possible.

---

# Canon

Every canonical relationship belongs to the Numeria Canon.

Stories may introduce new relationships.

They must never contradict established mathematical truth.

---

# Closing

Entities are the vocabulary of Numeria.

Relationships are its grammar.

Together they form a living universe of connected knowledge.
