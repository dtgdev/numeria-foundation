Page 5

Mr. Cobbler searched through the wooden box.

“Ben… Ben… Ben…”

At last, he pulled out a small card.

“Here it is!”

He placed the card on the table.

Written across the top was Ben’s name.

Below it were the measurements Mr. Cobbler had taken only yesterday.

Ben’s foot: 20 centimeters

Mr. Cobbler picked up his measuring board.

“Step here, please.”

Ben slipped off his shoe and placed his foot carefully on the board.

Mr. Cobbler moved the wooden marker to the end of Ben’s longest toe.

Then he stopped.

He checked it once.

He checked it twice.

He even cleaned his glasses and checked it a third time.

“That cannot be right,” he whispered.

“What does it say?” Ben asked.

Mr. Cobbler slowly turned the board toward them.

Ben’s foot: 22 centimeters

Ben’s mouth fell open.

“My foot grew two centimeters?”

Derivative looked at yesterday’s card.

Then at today’s measurement.

The Silver Delta grew warmer against his chest.

But it did not glow brightly.

Not yet.

Derivative opened his notebook.

He wrote:

Yesterday: 20 centimeters

Today: 22 centimeters

Change: 2 centimeters

Ben leaned closer.

“So my shoes didn’t shrink.”

Derivative shook his head.

“No.”

“Then my feet grew!”

“It certainly looks that way,” Derivative said.

Ben stared down at his toes.

“Why?”

Derivative closed his notebook.

“We have found what changed.”

He gently touched the Silver Delta.

“Now we must discover what caused it.”

Just then, something tiny fell from the bottom of Ben’s sock.

It landed on Mr. Cobbler’s table.

A silver leaf.

The Silver Delta flashed.
