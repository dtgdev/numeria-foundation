The silver leaf trembled on the table.

The Silver Delta flashed once.

Then twice.

Derivative leaned closer.

Tiny lines ran across the leaf like silver rivers.

Mr. Cobbler stepped back.

“I have never seen anything like that.”

Ben looked worried.

“I have.”

Derivative turned.

“Where?”

Ben stared at the floor.

For a moment, he said nothing.

Then he whispered,

“Yesterday, I went near the old forest.”

Mr. Cobbler’s face grew serious.

“The Everchange Forest?”

Ben nodded.

Derivative had heard stories about that place.

Plants there could grow overnight.

Vines could twist around trees before sunset.

Flowers could bloom, shrink, and bloom again before morning.

But no one in Numeria Village knew why.

“You were told not to enter that forest,” Mr. Cobbler said.

“I know,” Ben replied softly.

“I only went a little way inside.”

Derivative picked up the leaf carefully.

“What happened?”

Ben thought hard.

“I saw a strange silver plant.”

“It had one bright fruit hanging from it.”

Derivative’s fingers tightened around the pendant.

“Did you touch it?”

Ben looked away.

“I ate one small bite.”

The room became silent.

Derivative looked at Ben’s feet.

Then at the silver leaf.

Then at the glowing Delta around his neck.

“Ben,” he said gently, “your feet may not be finished changing.”

Ben’s eyes widened.

“What do you mean?”

Before Derivative could answer, Ben’s shoe made a tiny sound.

Crrrkkk.

A small split appeared near his longest toe.

The Silver Delta blazed with silver light.

Derivative opened his notebook.

At the top of a fresh page, he wrote:

The change is still happening.
