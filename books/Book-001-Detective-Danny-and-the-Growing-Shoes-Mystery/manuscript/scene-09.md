Final Page

Case Closed

Derivative carefully picked one blue flower.

The Silver Delta glowed softly.

Not brightly.

Not urgently.

Calmly.

As if it were saying:

This is the answer.

Derivative crushed one petal into a cup of clear water.

The water shimmered blue and silver.

Ben stared at it.

“Do I have to drink that?”

Derivative smiled.

“Only a tiny sip.”

Ben closed his eyes and drank.

For a moment…

nothing happened.

Then the Silver Delta became cool against Derivative’s chest.

The silver light around Ben’s feet slowly faded.

Mr. Cobbler measured again.

Twenty-two centimeters.

They waited.

Then measured once more.

Still…

Twenty-two centimeters.

Ben looked at Derivative.

“They stopped growing!”

Derivative smiled.

“The amount was changing.”

“But now the change has stopped.”

Ben looked down at his very large feet.

“Will they become normal again?”

Derivative laughed.

“That may take a little time.”

Mr. Cobbler held up an enormous new pair of shoes.

“Luckily,” he said proudly, “I make shoes in every size.”

Everyone laughed.

Derivative opened his notebook.

He wrote:

⸻

Case File #001

What changed?
Ben’s feet.

How fast did they change?
Two centimeters every day.

What caused the change?
The silver fruit from the Everchange Forest.

What stopped it?
The blue balancing flower.

Status:
CASE CLOSED

⸻

Ben pointed at the glowing pendant.

“How did you know my feet were changing by the same amount every day?”

Derivative held up the Silver Delta.

“It helped me see the pattern.”

“But the necklace did not solve the mystery.”

“You did,” he said.

Ben blinked.

“I did?”

Derivative nodded.

“You noticed the numbers.”

“You saw that two centimeters were being added again and again.”

“That is how every great discovery begins.”

“With noticing.”

Ben smiled.

Derivative closed his notebook and looked toward the Everchange Forest.

The Silver Delta rested quietly against his chest.

For now, the mystery was over.

But the world was still changing.

And somewhere in Numeria…

another clue was already waiting.

Derivative smiled.

“Keep your eyes open.”

“The world is always leaving clues.”

The End
