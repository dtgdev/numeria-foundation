The Magic of Derivative

Derivative held the Silver Delta tightly.

Its light spread across the shoe shop in thin, glowing lines.

One silver line touched Ben’s foot.

Another reached toward the mysterious leaf.

Then numbers began to appear in the air.

20 centimeters

22 centimeters

24 centimeters

Ben stared.

“What are those?”

Derivative watched carefully.

“Your measurements.”

The glowing numbers floated beside one another.

Yesterday:

20 centimeters

Today:

22 centimeters

And tomorrow, if the change continued:

24 centimeters

Mr. Cobbler gasped.

“You can see what will happen next?”

Derivative shook his head.

“The Silver Delta does not show me the future.”

“It shows me the pattern.”

He pointed toward the numbers.

“Ben’s feet are growing by two centimeters every day.”

Ben looked frightened.

“Will they keep growing forever?”

Derivative studied the silver leaf.

Then he remembered something his grandmother had once told him.

“Every unusual change has a beginning.
Find where it began, and you may discover how to stop it.”

Derivative turned the leaf over.

On its back was a tiny blue mark shaped like a raindrop.

He held the Silver Delta close to it.

The pendant flashed.

Suddenly, a map of glowing silver paths appeared across the leaf.

One path led deep into the Everchange Forest.

Derivative smiled.

“This leaf is not only a clue.”

“It is a map.”

“A map to what?” Ben asked.

Derivative placed the leaf carefully inside his notebook.

“To the plant you ate.”

Ben stared at his stretching shoes.

“But how will finding the plant help us?”

Derivative opened his notebook and wrote:

The change began with the silver fruit.

Then beneath it:

The solution may grow beside it.

Mr. Cobbler raised an eyebrow.

“Why would the solution be nearby?”

Derivative smiled.

“Because nature often keeps balance.”

“Where something causes change, something else may help control it.”

The Silver Delta glowed brighter than ever.

Derivative closed his notebook.

“We know what is changing.”

“We know how fast it is changing.”

“And now…”

He looked toward the distant forest.

“…we know where to search for the answer.”

Ben took a nervous breath.

“Are we really going back?”

Derivative placed a hand on his friend’s shoulder.

“You won’t go alone.”

Then he turned toward the door.

The silver pendant shone against his chest.

For the first time, Derivative was not merely following the mystery.

He was leading them toward its solution.
