The Silver Delta’s Trail

Derivative and Ben hurried toward the Everchange Forest.

The Silver Delta glowed against Derivative’s chest.

At every fork in the path, its light stretched in one direction like a thin silver thread.

“Is it showing us where to go?” Ben asked.

Derivative nodded.

“But remember—the Silver Delta does not give us answers.”

“It only guides us toward the clues.”

They followed the silver light beneath twisting branches and enormous leaves.

Soon they reached a clearing.

In the center stood the strange silver plant.

Its vines curled around an old tree.

Small glowing fruits hung from its branches.

Ben pointed.

“That’s it!”

“That’s the fruit I ate!”

Derivative stepped closer, but he did not touch it.

The Silver Delta began to pulse.

Once.

Then again.

Then again.

Each pulse came after exactly the same amount of time.

Derivative opened his notebook.

He studied Ben’s measurements.

Yesterday: 20 centimeters

Today: 22 centimeters

Tomorrow, if the pattern continues: 24 centimeters

Ben peered over his shoulder.

“It keeps adding two.”

Derivative nodded slowly.

“Two centimeters during the first day.”

“Two more during the second.”

“And if nothing stops it…”

“Two more every day,” Ben finished.

Derivative smiled.

“You found the pattern.”

Ben looked at the glowing fruit.

“So my feet are changing at the same speed every day?”

“Exactly.”

Derivative drew three footprints in his notebook.

The first was small.

The second was two centimeters longer.

The third was two centimeters longer again.

Beneath them, he wrote:

The amount changes.

The change stays the same.

The Silver Delta flashed brightly.

A beam of silver light swept across the clearing.

It passed over the glowing fruit…

then stopped on a tiny blue flower growing beneath the plant.

Ben pointed.

“Look!”

The flower’s petals opened and closed with every pulse of the Silver Delta.

Derivative knelt beside it.

On each blue petal was the same silver raindrop mark they had seen on the leaf.

“The changing fruit grows above,” Derivative whispered.

“And the balancing flower grows below.”

Ben’s eyes widened.

“Do you think the flower can stop my feet from growing?”

Derivative studied the plant.

“I think it may slow the change.”

“But we must test our idea carefully.”

He looked at Ben and smiled.

“A pattern helped us understand the problem.”

“Now another pattern may help us find the solution.”

The Silver Delta gave one final glow.

This time, its light did not point deeper into the forest.

It pointed directly at the blue flower.
