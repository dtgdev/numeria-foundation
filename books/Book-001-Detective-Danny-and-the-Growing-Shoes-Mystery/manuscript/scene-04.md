The tiny bell above the shoe shop door jingled.

Jingle… Jingle…

“Good morning!” called a cheerful voice.

Mr. Cobbler looked up from his workbench.

He was polishing a shiny brown boot until it sparkled like honey in the sunshine.

“Derivative!” he said with a smile.

“And Ben! What brings my two favorite visitors here this morning?”

Ben lifted one foot.

“My shoes don’t fit.”

Mr. Cobbler chuckled.

“That can’t be.”

“I made those shoes myself.”

“And I’m very good at making shoes.”

Derivative smiled politely.

“We know.”

Mr. Cobbler winked.

“The best shoes in all of Numeria Village.”

Ben tried to smile.

“But they hurt.”

Mr. Cobbler’s smile slowly faded.

“Hmmm…”

“Let’s have a look.”

He carefully untied Ben’s shoe.

Then he gently slipped it off.

He turned it over.

He looked inside.

He bent the leather.

He checked the stitching.

He even sniffed it.

Derivative watched every movement without saying a word.

Finally Mr. Cobbler stood up.

“The shoe is perfect.”

Ben frowned.

“But it doesn’t fit.”

“I know.”

Mr. Cobbler scratched his beard.

“Which means…”

He looked at the shoe.

Then at Ben’s foot.

Then back at the shoe again.

Derivative noticed something.

Mr. Cobbler wasn’t guessing anymore.

He was thinking.

Very carefully.

Derivative smiled.

The Silver Delta resting against his chest became just a little warmer.

Mr. Cobbler walked slowly to the back of the shop.

When he returned, he was carrying an old wooden box.

He placed it gently on the table.

The brass latch clicked open.

Inside were hundreds of neatly written cards.

Derivative’s eyes grew wide.

“What are those?”

Mr. Cobbler smiled proudly.

“My measurements.”

“I’ve kept every customer’s measurements since the day I opened this shop.”

He looked at Ben.

“If your shoes don’t fit…”

“…perhaps the mystery isn’t the shoes at all.”
