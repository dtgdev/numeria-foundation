The World of Numeria

Status: Foundational
Project: Project Lantern
Purpose: Canonical world-building structure

Overview

This directory contains the canonical world model for Numeria.

Numeria is not a decorative fantasy setting placed around mathematics. It is a world whose places, institutions, cultures, artifacts, and journeys emerge from mathematical ideas.

Every part of the world must strengthen at least one of the following:

* Mathematical understanding
* Learner curiosity
* Story potential
* Visual memory
* Educational progression

Core Principle

Every place in Numeria must exist for a mathematical reason.

Locations should never be added merely because they sound imaginative.

A location earns its place when it helps learners understand, experience, visualize, or remember a mathematical idea.

Responsibilities

WORLD_FOUNDATIONS.md

Defines the enduring laws and philosophy of the Numeria universe.

It explains:

* Why Numeria exists
* How mathematics shapes reality
* Who the citizens are
* Who the learner is
* How knowledge, discovery, and stories interact

WORLD_ATLAS.md

Defines the major regions of Numeria and how they relate to mathematical domains.

It serves as the high-level map of the world.

domains/

Contains the canonical descriptions of major mathematical regions.

Initial domains include:

* Numbers
* Algebra
* Geometry
* Calculus
* Probability
* Statistics
* Logic

locations/

Contains cities, valleys, forests, observatories, bridges, rivers, frontiers, and other meaningful places.

Every location must map to mathematical knowledge.

institutions/

Contains guilds, academies, councils, bureaus, libraries, laboratories, and organizations.

Institutions create recurring settings for stories and educational experiences.

artifacts/

Contains meaningful objects used by Explorers and Numeria citizens.

Examples may include:

* The Numeria Compass
* Explorer Passport
* Notebook of Clues
* Magnifying Lens of Change

Artifacts should serve as learning metaphors rather than arbitrary magical objects.

travel/

Defines how learners move through Numeria.

Travel may represent:

* Prerequisites
* Learning progression
* Curiosity-driven exploration
* Cross-domain relationships
* Increasing mathematical depth

Relationship to Canonical Knowledge

World elements must reference canonical Numeria identifiers whenever possible.

Examples:

NUM-CON-000001
NUM-CHR-000001
NUM-LOC-000001
NUM-ORG-000001
NUM-ART-000001

The world may dramatize mathematical truth, but it must never redefine it.

The Learner

The learner is the hero of Numeria.

Learners enter the world as Explorers.

Numeria characters act as guides, partners, mentors, investigators, and companions.

They help learners discover answers rather than solving every problem for them.

World-Building Test

Before adding a new world element, ask:

1. What mathematical idea does it represent?
2. What educational purpose does it serve?
3. What stories could happen there?
4. What should learners remember about it?
5. Does it belong in the existing world?
6. Can it support more than one experience?

If these questions cannot be answered clearly, the world element should be redesigned.

Guiding Promise

Numeria should feel adventurous, coherent, welcoming, and alive.

A learner should never feel that they are merely moving through a disguised textbook.

They should feel that they are exploring a world where every mystery reveals a deeper pattern.
