Overview

The World Atlas provides the high-level organization of Numeria.

Rather than dividing the world by countries or political boundaries, Numeria is organized around major mathematical domains. Each domain represents a family of related ideas and serves as the home for its own characters, institutions, locations, and adventures.

Together, these domains form a single connected world where Explorers journey through mathematics by exploring meaningful places and solving engaging mysteries.

The Domains

Kingdom of Numbers

The birthplace of counting, arithmetic, and numerical intuition.

Explorers begin their journey here.

⸻

Algebra Province

The land of patterns, variables, equations, and relationships.

Explorers learn to describe the unknown.

⸻

Geometry Province

A world of shapes, space, symmetry, measurement, and visual reasoning.

⸻

Data Sciences Province

Home to probability, statistics, data, uncertainty, and evidence-based thinking.

⸻

Calculus Province

The province of change, motion, accumulation, optimization, and continuous thinking.

Detective Derivative and the Calculus Guild reside here.

⸻

Infinity Frontier

The ever-expanding horizon of mathematics.

A place representing exploration, unanswered questions, advanced ideas, and lifelong learning.

It reminds every Explorer that mathematics is never truly finished.

⸻

How Explorers Travel

Explorers are free to travel between domains.

Journeys are guided by curiosity, readiness, and learning goals rather than rigid pathways.

Some adventures span multiple domains, showing how mathematical ideas connect.

⸻

Guilds

Each domain contains one or more guilds.

Guilds are communities of characters who dedicate themselves to understanding and protecting particular mathematical ideas.

Examples include:

* Calculus Guild
* Algebra Guild
* Geometry Guild
* Probability Guild
* Statistics Society

Additional guilds may be introduced as Numeria grows.

⸻

Cities and Landmarks

Every domain contains meaningful locations.

Examples include:

* Differential City
* Integral Valley
* Function Forest
* Equation Square
* Probability Harbor
* Limit Cliffs

Each location exists because it reinforces a mathematical idea and provides opportunities for stories and learning.

⸻

The Explorer’s Journey

Every learner enters Numeria as an Explorer.

The journey begins in the Kingdom of Numbers and expands through increasingly rich mathematical experiences.

Explorers do not simply complete lessons.

They investigate mysteries, meet guides, visit new places, and gradually build confidence through discovery.

No two journeys need to be identical.

Curiosity determines the path.

⸻

Future Growth

The World Atlas is designed to grow.

New mathematical domains, locations, institutions, artifacts, characters, and adventures may be added over time, provided they remain faithful to the World Foundations and the Charter of the Numeria Foundation.

Every addition should strengthen the coherence of Numeria and enrich the learner’s experience.
